Mall Monuments Walk
~13:15–15:30 · Grab and go if peckish — food trucks on the Mall

Walk west from the White House along the Mall. Washington Monument first — worth standing at the base even without going up. Then 5 min to the WWII Memorial — fountains and state pillars, often missed but moving. Then west along the Reflecting Pool to Lincoln Memorial. Immediately right: Vietnam Veterans Memorial — 58,000 names on black granite. 5 min south: Korea War Veterans Memorial — ghostly steel soldiers.

Allow ~2 hours for the full walk at a relaxed pace. Food trucks and kiosks line the Mall if you need something to eat on the move.

All free · All open 24h Washington Monument inside: book at recreation.gov

Georgetown
~15:45–19:00 · Uber from Lincoln Memorial ~10 min

Take an Uber from Lincoln Memorial rather than walking — you'll have earned it by then. Georgetown Waterfront Park is right there for a stroll along the Potomac. Then M Street & Wisconsin Ave for a browse. The Exorcist Steps (36th St at Prospect St NW) if you fancy a quick detour — five minutes and a decent photo. Dumbarton Oaks gardens close at 18:00 in May if you want greenery.

Practical Tips
💡
Getting Around DC

Metro — Clean, safe, simple. SmarTrip works like Oyster. ~$2–7 per journey. Silver Line connects to Dulles.

Walking — The Mall is deceptively long. Allow 15–20 min between monuments. Comfortable shoes non-negotiable for museum and monument days.

Uber/Lyft — Best for Georgetown (no Metro station). Also good for evenings when feet give out. Cheaper than NYC.

🌡️
Late May Weather

Expect 22–28°C and humid. Comfortable in the morning, sticky by afternoon. Sunscreen and a water bottle essential on Mall days. Evenings are pleasant — perfect for the monuments walk.
